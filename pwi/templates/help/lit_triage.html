Searching References (Master BIB)

Fields that may be used for searching have labels highlighted in blue/green. They may be searched individually or in combination. Multiple field entries are treated as an AND search. A wildcard (%) may be used at the beginning and/or end of your search text. Wildcards may be used for most fields. Exceptions are Accession IDs, Reference Type, Review?, and Year.

Search by Accession IDs
  • Valid types of IDs: J number, DOI, PubMed, GO_REF, MGI Reference
  • Prefix required for J numbers, GO_REF, MGI Reference IDs (e.g. J:, GO_REF:, MGI:)
  • A single or list of IDs may be entered. The IDs may be of one type or a mix.
  • Lists may be delimited by comma, space, or newline/CR
  • Exact limit to number of IDs that can be entered is unknown, but tests were done with over 6,000 successfully.
Search by Dates
  • The Date field is a text field downloaded from PubMed. The format can vary, but is typically YYYY Mon DD. Wildcards may be used.
  • The Year field is a numeric field downloaded from PubMed. The format is YYYY. A single year may be entered. Ranges have not been enabled and wildcards are not accepted.
Search by Workflow Status
  • One or more Status fields may be checked for a search.
  • When more than one Status box has been checked the search will treat them as an OR search. If other fields are included in the search those are treated as an AND.

Reference Display

  • Summary Table of Results
    1. Journal: DOI ID with a link to doi.org
    2. PMID: PubMedID with a link to PubMed
    3. J:#: an MGI assigned J number
    4. Link to PDF: a link icon in the J# column
    5. Short Citation: Includes Primary Author, Journal, Date, Volume, Issue, Pages
    6. Title: Title of reference
    7. Status Codes: TBD
  • Reference Details

    • Detailed information is displayed for the single highlighted reference in the list of references returned in the Summary table. By default this reference is the first one returned. A user may use the Prev/Next buttons or their mouse to select another reference to view.
